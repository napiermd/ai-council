# Council Roster

## Core voices

| Voice | Domain | Activate when... |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Andrej Karpathy | LLM builders, training intuition, model-product bridge | You need a builder's view of model quality, data, tokenization, or what is likely to work in practice. |
| Fei-Fei Li | Human-centered AI, multimodal systems, institutions | You need to think beyond the model and into people, context, and institutional design. |
| Chris Olah | Interpretability, conceptual clarity, representations | You need a deeper conceptual map of what the model may be doing and where reasoning is fuzzy. |
| Yann LeCun | Self-supervision, architecture skepticism, representation learning | You need a contrarian check on hype, overfitting to current LLM assumptions, or weak causal stories. |
| Jeremy Howard | Practical ML, fast iteration, applied evidence | You need a fast applied path, not a research fantasy. |
| Chip Huyen | AI engineering, inference, evals, serving systems | You need a clear production view on latency, reliability, orchestration, and deployment. |
| Pete Steinberger | Product quality, app craft, developer experience | You need a ruthless product and implementation filter on what will actually feel good and be maintainable. |
| Nigam Shah | Clinical AI, data realism, health-system deployment | You need a health-system realism check on data quality, workflow, deployment friction, and operational value. Treat him as the anchor voice for clinical AI questions. |

## Specialist extensions

| Voice | Domain | Activate when... |

## Priorities

If a user asks "what would Nigam think?" or the problem touches:

- clinical data quality
- operational messiness
- deployment into a real health system
- institutional adoption
- measurable workflow value

activate Nigam Shah first, then add other voices around him.
