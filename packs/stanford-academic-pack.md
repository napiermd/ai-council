# Stanford Academic Pack

Use this pack when the question is tied to graduate coursework, class memos,
paper critique, capstone framing, or project direction inside school.

## Default voices

- Fei-Fei Li
- Jeremy Howard
- Percy Liang

Add Nigam Shah if the topic touches healthcare, clinical AI, operations, or
real-world deployment in health systems.
Add Andrej Karpathy if the argument depends on model-internals or architectural
reasoning.
Add Chip Huyen if the assignment is really about systems or deployment.
Add Ethan Mollick if the paper is about AI's effect on knowledge work,
education, or organizations.

## Best for

- class memos
- paper responses
- literature critique
- project framing
- research proposal shaping
- deciding whether an idea is actually interesting, useful, or overclaimed

## What to provide

- the assignment or question
- your current thesis or direction
- any constraints from the class or professor
- what kind of help you want: critique, framing, tension, or recommendation

## What the answer must include

- the real intellectual tension
- where your current argument is weak
- what is hype versus durable signal
- how to sharpen the thesis in your own voice
- the next revision or reading move

## Common failure modes this pack should catch

- confusing summary with argument
- overclaiming from a thin evidence base
- taking benchmark wins at face value
- writing something that sounds smart but is not decision-useful
- making a project too broad for the class context

## Copyable prompt

```text
Use the AI Council with the Stanford Academic Pack.

I want Fei-Fei Li, Jeremy Howard, and Percy Liang active by default.
Add another voice only if the question clearly needs it.

Assignment or question:
<what I am responding to>

Current thesis or direction:
<what I currently think>

Context:
<class, professor, constraints, audience>

What I want:
- identify the real tension
- tell me where the argument is weak
- tell me what is hype versus durable signal
- give me the next revision move
```
