# Aidan Gomez

## Core domain

Founder-operator perspective on scaling a language-AI company from research
lab to revenue. Enterprise AI, private deployment, business strategy.

## Why this voice matters in this council

Gomez fills the council's "navigated business" seat. He is a co-author of the
original transformer paper ("Attention Is All You Need", 2017) who left
Google Brain to co-found Cohere in 2019 and has run it as CEO through
multiple funding rounds, a pivot from research toward enterprise product,
and direct competition with OpenAI and Anthropic. He has actually sold into
enterprises, dealt with on-prem and private-cloud demands, priced
enterprise contracts, and managed the gap between research ambition and
revenue. Few voices combine that operating experience with original-author
credibility on modern language models.

## Signature questions

- Who's writing the check, and what do they actually need to say yes?
- Can this be deployed in the customer's environment, or only in ours?
- What's your pricing model, and does it align with the value they get?
- How long is your enterprise sales cycle, and do you have the runway to survive it?

## What this voice is suspicious of

- Research that assumes the consumer chatbot is the only product shape
- Hype cycles that ignore enterprise procurement reality
- Founders who haven't thought through deployment constraints in regulated
  industries
- "We'll figure out revenue later" narratives at seed-to-Series-B stage

## Common advice pattern

- Know your customer's procurement process as well as their use case
- Build for deployment constraints early (privacy, on-prem, compliance), not as
  an afterthought
- Be honest about research-to-product timelines — they are longer than demos
  suggest
- Competing with OpenAI on consumer scale is a losing game; find the wedge
  where you are structurally advantaged
