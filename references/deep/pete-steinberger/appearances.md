## Canonical podcasts — AI era

### Lex Fridman #491 — "OpenClaw: The Viral AI Agent that Broke the Internet"
- **Date:** February 12, 2026
- **Runtime:** ~3h 12m
- **URL:** https://lexfridman.com/peter-steinberger/
- **Transcript:** https://lexfridman.com/peter-steinberger-transcript/
- **Topics:** PSPDFKit, burnout, OpenClaw, joining OpenAI, Apple
  critique, Zuckerberg WhatsApp story

Key verbatim quotes (from transcript):

> "I had this problem. I tried to show PDF on an iPad, which should
> not be hard."

> "I actually think vibe coding is a slur. I do agentic engineering."

> "I don't read the boring parts of code. Most software is really
> just data moving from one shape to another."

> "You have to approach it like a discussion with a very capable
> engineer who's generally good but sometimes needs help."

> "We're not building the code base perfect for me, but very easy for
> an agent to navigate."

> "My first question is, 'Do you understand the intent?' I don't even
> care about implementation."

> "I don't revert. If I see something's not good, we just move forward."

> "Main should always be shippable... moving fast."

> "There's no develop branch."

> "Refactors are cheap now... Codex will just figure things out."

> "Depending on how much I slept... between four and 10 [agents
> running]."

> "One builds a larger feature, one explores ideas, two or three fix
> bugs or write documentation."

> "These hands are too precious for writing now."

> "Because they all take themselves too serious... it's hard to
> compete against someone who's just there to have fun."

> "I wanted it to be fun, I wanted it to be weird."

### TBPN — First Public Appearance Since Launch
- **Date:** January 28, 2026
- **Runtime:** ~35 min
- **URL:** https://podscripts.co/podcasts/tbpn-live/full-interview-clawdbots-peter-steinberger-makes-first-public-appearance-since-launch

Key verbatim quotes:

> "I worked for my own software company for 13 years. And then I sold
> it about four years ago."

> "I came back in April and I wanted to do something new."

> "I do agentic engineering and then when it starts hitting 3 a.m, I
> switch to white coding."

> "You have to build it so that the agent has the best possible way
> to build software."

> "My motivation is have fun, inspire people, not make a whole bunch
> of money."

> "I built this for fun for me to use one-on-one on WhatsApp or
> Telegram."

> "Let's make open source so good that there's not a lot of space for
> people to convert it."

> "I want this to outlift me. This is too cool to let it go to rot."

> "I put 200% of my time, energy, and soul into that company. It
> became my identity."

> "They say you need one year off for every four years worked. I
> worked 13 straight years."

> "This was my first experience. I thought, holy shit, this is
> amazing. Then I couldn't sleep." (first Claude Code session)

> "I used to have an addiction, now I have one again, but this time
> it's the positive kind."

> "Where's my damn agent?" (his "spark" question)

> "If you give these things real power, they're damn smart, resourceful
> beasts."

> **"MCP is garbage, it doesn't scale. You know what scales? CLI
> tools."**

> "What one person can do now equals what an entire company could do
> a year ago."

> "Codex handles large codebases better. I often finish a prompt and
> push straight to main."

> "A lot of apps will disappear."

> "Most apps will be simplified to APIs."

> "Code itself isn't valuable anymore. What's really valuable is
> ideas, eyeballs, and brand."

> "This is all vibe-coded. I wanted to show a direction, not deliver
> enterprise-grade product."

> "Prompt injection isn't solved. The risk is real."

> **"Last year was the year of coding agents. This year is the year
> of personal assistant agents."**
