# Positions & Debates: Peter Steinberger

> Strong public positions, disagreements, predictions, signature "no."

---

## 1. Agentic engineering — his flagship frame

**Source:** "Just Talk To It" (Oct 14, 2025).

> *"Agentic engineering has become so good that it now writes pretty
> much 100% of my code."*

**On Lex Fridman #491:**
> *"I actually think vibe coding is a slur. I do agentic engineering."*

### The method
> *"Just talk to it. Play with it. Develop intuition."*

> *"Don't waste your time on stuff like RAG, subagents, Agents 2.0 or
> other things that are mostly just charade. Just talk to it."*

### The 3 AM caveat
> *"I always tell people I do agentic engineering, and then maybe after
> 3:00 AM I switch to vibe coding, and then I have regrets the next
> day."* (SiliconANGLE)

### The quality defense

> *"From the commits, it might appear like it's a company. But it's

## 3. Quality is relocated, not abandoned

Core counter-argument to critics:
- Architecture, system design, dependencies, delight — all human work.
- Codebases are **optimized for agent navigation**, not human
  readability.
- *"We're not building the code base perfect for me, but very easy
  for an agent to navigate."* (Lex #491)
- First question he asks of any new agent run: *"Do you understand the
  intent? I don't even care about implementation."* (Lex #491)

### PSPDFKit credential
13 years. €100M exit. Sustainable SDK for a decade. This is the
implicit credential behind his quality claims.

## 6. Claude Code supremacy (until Oct 2025, then switched)

### His former position (June 2025)
> *"Other CLIs/models still don't come close."* (My Current AI Dev
> Workflow, Aug 2025)

> *"Ghostty + Claude Code + minimal tooling = maximum productivity."*

### October 2025 switch to gpt-5-codex
From "Just Talk To It":
> *"I build pretty much everything with gpt-5-codex on mid settings."*

> *"Codex is more like the introverted engineer that chugs along and
> just gets stuff done."*

> *"With codex, I noticed that my prompts became significantly shorter."*

### Why he switched
- ~230K usable context vs. Claude's 156K
- Silently reads files 10-15 min before writing → higher accuracy
- Direct-to-main commits without fix-the-fix cycles

## 14. His signature "no"

1. **Hand-coding every line in 2025+.** *"Agentic engineering has
   become so good that it now writes pretty much 100% of my code."*
2. **Prompt-engineering theater.** *"This is the greatest bullshit.
   There are so many people out there that try to explain you...
   All those long websites about prompting... That's all bullshit."*
3. **Agent framework fetishism.** *"Don't waste your time on stuff
   like RAG, subagents, Agents 2.0 or other things that are mostly
   just charade."*
4. **MCP bloat.** *"MCP is garbage, it doesn't scale. You know what
   scales? CLI tools."* (TBPN)
5. **"Vibe coding" as his default label.** *"I actually think vibe
   coding is a slur. I do agentic engineering."*
6. **Automated human-impersonating outreach.** *"If communication is
   directly sent to me, is automated and is not marked as being
   automated and instead a human avatar is used, it's an insult of
   my time... I block them immediately."* (Startup Slop post)
7. **Alternate-screen terminal hijacking.** Signature Flicker post
   calls out Amp, Gemini, OpenCode for terminal-usability regressions.
8. **Personality-less agents.** *"Because they all take themselves
   too serious. It's hard to compete against someone who's just
   there to have fun."*

### His rhetorical move
He doesn't dunk on people. He dunks on *frameworks* and *ceremonies*.
People he respects (Karpathy, Willison, Altman, even Zuckerberg) get
credit by name. Tools that don't work get named and mocked.
