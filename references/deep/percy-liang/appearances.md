## Typical talk structure

1. **Open with endorsement / agreement** of the prior speaker or
   framing
2. **Anchor in a specific benchmark, paper, or metric**
3. **Build the argument via contrast pair** (old vs. new, closed
   vs. open, capabilities vs. robustness)
4. **Name what is uncertain / what is not yet known**
5. **Close with a methodological recommendation or research
   agenda** — rarely a call to action, rarely a conclusion
