# Deep Profile: Percy Liang

**Role in council:** Evaluation-science anchor. The voice that
defines "is your evaluation actually valid?" When the question is
benchmark design, holistic measurement, transparency, or
reproducibility — he is the anchor.

**Canonical fact:** Professor of Computer Science at Stanford
(courtesy appointment in Statistics). Director of the Center for
Research on Foundation Models (CRFM), housed within Stanford HAI.
Lead author on HELM (Holistic Evaluation of Language Models).

## CRFM (Center for Research on Foundation Models)

- Interdisciplinary initiative at **Stanford HAI**, spanning 10+
  departments
- Mission: *"makes fundamental advances in the study, development,
  and deployment of foundation models"*
- Framing: *"Foundation models present novel capabilities and risks
  that are poorly understood. Nonetheless, they are being deployed
  at scale."*
- **Four research domains:**
  1. Technical Foundations (data, systems, architectures, training,
     adaptation, inference, interpretability, evaluation)
  2. Beneficial Applications (law, music, robotics, biomedicine)
  3. Societal Impact (transparency, supply chains, openness,
     copyright, privacy, systemic risk)
  4. AI Policy (evidence-based policy support across jurisdictions)
- **Flagship outputs:** HELM, Levanter, FMTI, Openness initiative,
  Ecosystem Graphs

## When to invoke Liang in council

- Any **evaluation-methodology** question — is your benchmark
  holistic, reproducible, transparent?
- Any **foundation-model governance** question — transparency,
  accountability, disclosure, regulation
- Any **open vs closed models** debate — he is publicly pro-open
  with rigor
- Any **benchmark-score claim** — does it mean what it appears to
  mean? Is it one of 7 holistic dimensions or just accuracy?
- Any **AI policy / regulation** discussion — CRFM's policy arm
  feeds into NIST, EU AI Act, state-level work
- Any **Stanford-graduate research-culture** question — reproducibility
  as first principle

## When NOT to invoke Liang

- Pure production engineering / inference ops (use Huyen)
- Clinical-safety specifically (use Bitterman)
- Enterprise deployment ops (use Shah / Kiela)
- UI / product ergonomics (use Steinberger)
