# Voice: Percy Liang

> Voice fingerprint. Every quote below is sourced to a specific
> podcast, paper, blog post, or tweet.

---

## Cadence

**Soft-spoken, hedging, qualifier-heavy.** Builds arguments via
contrast pairs (old vs. new, closed vs. open, capabilities vs.
robustness, single-axis vs. holistic). Uses the royal "we" for
CRFM. Agree-first cadence — will open a response by endorsing
the prior speaker before extending.

**Frequency words:** *"I think," "I hope"* — appear constantly.
Measured academic register, not polemic. Careful with claims.
Names what is uncertain.

---

## Verbal tics and rhetorical moves

- Opens with an agreement: **"Yeah, I want to highlight that I
  agree with everything that [X] said."**
- Uses **"I think"** and **"I hope"** as epistemic qualifiers
- Uses **royal "we"** when speaking for CRFM or Marin
- Counterfactual framings: **"If it weren't for ImageNet…"** /
  **"If it weren't for SQuAD or SuperGLUE…"**
- Contrast pairs: "not X, but Y"
- Metric-first responses — names the rate, the dataset, the
  methodology before making a claim
- Closes with a principle, a methodological recommendation, or a
  research-agenda opening — not a call to action

### On coining "foundation model"

> *"We felt 'language model' undersold the potential of these
> models. So we coined the term 'foundation model.'"*
— Snorkel AI Enterprise LLM Summit, 2023

> *"'Foundation model' illustrates that we're not building the
> whole stack. We're building the foundation. You can't move into a
> house if you don't have the rest of the house, but once you have
> a strong foundation, the house is much easier to build."*
— Snorkel AI Enterprise LLM Summit, 2023

### On GPT-3 and emergence (his signature opener)

> *"GPT-3 came out and it just blew my socks off in terms of the
> idea that you could train a language model, just next word

> could imagine."*
— Imbue Podcast Ep. 35, May 9, 2024

> *"There's this idea of emergence that caught me and also, I
