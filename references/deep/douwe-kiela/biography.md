## Training path

### Undergraduate
- **Utrecht University** — BSc, Liberal Arts & Sciences, **double
  major in Cognitive Artificial Intelligence and Philosophy**

This combination — cognitive AI + philosophy — is important context
for how he frames LLM behavior. He comes at groundedness and
hallucination as *epistemic* problems, not just engineering ones.

### Masters
- **University of Amsterdam, Institute for Logic, Language and
  Computation (ILLC)** — MSc in Logic, **cum laude**

The ILLC is one of Europe's premier logic-and-language research
institutes. This cements the formal-semantics grounding underneath
his NLP work.

### PhD

  MPhil + PhD in CS, specializing in natural language processing and

## Career

### Facebook AI Research / FAIR (2016–~2021)
- Joined as **postdoctoral researcher** in the New York office
- Promoted to **Research Scientist**
- Built a portfolio of co-authored and first-authored NLP papers
  during this period. Worked closely with Sebastian Riedel, Tim
  Rocktäschel, Mike Lewis, and other FAIR NLP leadership
- **2020 — the RAG paper.** He was the PI / team lead (senior/last
  author). Patrick Lewis was the implementation lead (first author).
  The paper defined retrieval-augmented generation and is now the
  single most-cited paper on his Scholar profile (19,458 citations
  as of April 2026)
- **2020 — Hateful Memes Challenge.** First-authored NeurIPS paper
  introducing a multimodal hate-speech benchmark designed so
  "unimodal models struggle and only multimodal models can
  succeed"
- **2021 — Dynabench.** First-authored NAACL paper introducing a
  human-and-model-in-the-loop dynamic benchmarking platform. His
  signature contribution to evaluation methodology.
