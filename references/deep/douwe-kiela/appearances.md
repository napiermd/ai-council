## Typical talk structure

1. **Frame the meme or false dichotomy.** ("People say RAG is
   dead.")
2. **Rebut with the real question.** ("But the real question is,
   how do you give LLMs the right context?")
3. **Reframe as a systems question.** ("The model is 10–20% of the
   system.")
4. **Ground in an enterprise reality.** (HSBC, Qualcomm, Fortune 500.)
5. **Signature analogy.** (Harry Potter / headmaster; disk / RAM /
   network card.)
6. **Close with a principle.** ("You need to build for production,
   not for the demo.")

---

## Signature positions reinforced in talks

- **Context engineering as infrastructure category**
- **RAG + long context, not RAG *vs* long context**
- **Systems > models**
- **Specialization > generalization (for enterprise)**
- **Groundedness > raw accuracy**
- **Attribution as default, not feature**
- **Workflows become agentic over time**
