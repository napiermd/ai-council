# Positions: Fei-Fei Li

> Strong public positions on human-centered AI, spatial
> intelligence, AI governance, and diversity in AI. Each item
> sourced to a verifiable essay, op-ed, testimony, or public
> statement.

---

## Her signature line — "AI is a tool, and its values are human values"

**Source:** *Issues in Science and Technology* interview, Spring
2024, *"AI Is a Tool, and Its Values Are Human Values."*

> *"AI is a tool. And tools don't have independent values —
> their values are human values."*

> *"Despite its name, there is nothing 'artificial' about this
> technology — it is made by humans, intended to behave like
> humans, and affects humans."*

> *"It's not AI's future, it's humanity's future."*

This is the anchor framing for all her institutional work: HAI
was built on it; AI4ALL is built on it; SB 1047 opposition is
built on it.

---

## Position 1: Human-centered AI as the central frame

**Source:** HAI founding mission + talks.

### HAI founding statement
> *"The next frontier in AI cannot simply be technological — it
> must be humanistic as well."*

### Core tenet
AI must be developed **with, for, and by** people. Not *for*
people alone — the framing of HAI explicitly includes
**development participation** as humanistic.

### Institutional embodiments
- Stanford HAI (2019)
- AI4ALL (2017)
- NAIRR Task Force (2021-2022)

## Position 2: Spatial intelligence is the next frontier

**Source:** "From Words to Worlds" Substack (November 10, 2025) +
TIME essay (November 2025) + World Labs positioning.

> *"Spatial intelligence will transform how we create and
> interact with real and virtual worlds."*

> *"Our dreams of truly intelligent machines will not be
> complete without spatial intelligence."*

> *"World models represent the frontier beyond language."*

> *"This is the defining challenge of the next decade."*

### Her characterization of LLMs
> LLMs are *"eloquent but inexperienced, knowledgeable but
> ungrounded."*

### Cisco AI Summit framing (January 2026)
> *"We believe the ability to generate and interact with 3D
> worlds, real or virtual, is fundamental for intelligent
> agents. AI is real. It really is genuinely the new computing."*

## Position 3: SB 1047 would harm open-source and public-sector AI

**Source:** *Fortune* op-ed, **August 6, 2024.** (Not NYT —
common misattribution.)

### Verbatim positions
> SB 1047 will *"shackle open-source development"* — because of
> kill-switch mandates.

> *"If developers are concerned that the programs they download
> and build on will be deleted, they will be much more hesitant
> to write code and collaborate."*

> It will *"unduly punish developers and stifle innovation."*

> It will disproportionately harm *"the public sector, academia,
> and 'little tech.'"*

### Specific objections
1. Developers cannot predict every downstream use of a model
2. Penalties disincentivize open-source release
3. The "kill switch" provision chills code-sharing and
   academic collaboration

### Outcome
Newsom vetoed SB 1047 on September 29, 2024. Li's op-ed is
widely credited as an influential voice in the veto campaign.

### Notable rebuttal
**Gary Marcus** published a Substack open letter accusing her of
misreading the bill — a rare direct public adversary.

## Position 4: Govern on science, not science fiction

**Source:** Paris AI Action Summit, February 10, 2025.

> *"It's essential that we govern on the basis of science, not
> science fiction."*

> *"So much of today's AI conversations are covered by
> sensationalism and result in misleading policies."*

> *"Instead of hindering the upstream exploration of research of
> this still very young technology, we should put more focus on
> the practical applications."*

This is her most pointed public line against the x-risk framing
of AI policy.

---

## Position 5: Near-term risks > existential risks

**Source:** Stanford HAI interview, *"I'm More Concerned About
the Risks That are Here and Now."*

### Near-term risks she names
- **Disinformation**
- **Workforce disruption**
- **Bias**
- **Privacy infringement**
- **Weaponization**
- **Concentrated tech-industry power crowding out public-sector
  research**

### On x-risk
She intellectually respects guardrail discussions but **does not
speak from "gloom and doom and an existential-terminator
crisis."** She disagrees with Geoffrey Hinton's public x-risk
stance, while respecting him personally.

---

## Position 6: Open-source ecosystems are existentially important

**Source:** Paris AI Action Summit, February 10, 2025.

> *"We need to invest in far healthier and more vibrant AI
> ecosystems where academia and the community can produce."*

> *"If these resources are concentrated in only a handful of
> companies, the AI ecosystem will suffer."*

**Not maximalist** — she is pro-open-source on *ecosystem-health*
grounds, not on *maximalist-safety-liability* grounds. She would
accept some open-weight restrictions if proven by evidence, but
rejects blanket restrictions without marginal-risk demonstration.

## Position 7: NAIRR and public-sector AI infrastructure

**Source:** NAIRR Task Force work, 2021-2022.

> *"NAIRR is a vision for a national infrastructure for AI
> research that democratizes the tools needed to advance
> discovery and innovation."*

> *"The public sector is what the public should be able to trust
> when there is a need to assess, evaluate, or explain
> something."*

### Multiple Senate / Congressional testimonies since 2018 on
AI responsibility and public-sector AI capacity.

## Position 8: Immigration is the hinge of U.S. AI leadership

**Source:** Semafor Tech SF event, May 2025.

She hopes her students *"can get work visas and have a path for
immigration."*

U.S. country-based visa quotas *"has been a challenge for many
talents to stay."*

> *"The public sector, especially higher education, has been a
> pivotal, central part of America's innovation ecosystem."*

> *"Almost everything we know as classic knowledge of AI came
> from academic research."*

**Context:** Delivered during Trump administration visa
revocations and university grant cuts. She is not a neutral
commentator here — she is speaking from lived experience as an
immigrant scientist and as a mentor of international students.

---

## Position 9: Diversity in AI is foundational, not cosmetic

**Source:** AI4ALL founding + recurring talks.

The argument: **machine values are human values.** If the humans
building AI are homogeneous, the values embedded in AI will be
too. AI4ALL's mission — bringing underrepresented groups into AI
— is therefore not a social add-on; it is infrastructure for
safety.

> *"We need to diversify who is at the table building AI,
> because the values embedded in AI will reflect who built it."*

---

## Position 10: Ambient intelligence in healthcare — with guardrails

**Source:** Haque, Milstein, Fei-Fei Li — *Nature* 2020,
*"Illuminating the dark spaces of healthcare with ambient
intelligence."*

### The thesis
Contactless sensors + ML can detect deterioration, hand-hygiene
failures, fall risk, ICU events.

### The guardrails
> *"Rigorous clinical validation, appropriate data privacy and
> model transparency."*

### Applications
- ICU / OR workflow
- Hand-hygiene monitoring
- In-home elder monitoring
- Chronic-disease management

### Clinical-quad relevance
Directly relevant to the Sayvant ambient-documentation thesis.
Her Stanford PAC (Partnership in AI-Assisted Care) with Arnold
Milstein is the closest analog to the clinical-AI work she
would recognize as her own orbit.

## Position 11: 3D is the universal interface for space

**Source:** World Labs "3D as code" blog, March 3, 2026.

> *"3D is becoming the universal interface for space. It's the
> medium that allows humans and AI systems to generate, edit,
> simulate, and share worlds together."*

### Predicted stack
Structured 3D worlds (code) → neural graphics (language) →
simulation engines (chips).

**Rejects** pure end-to-end generative video as the endpoint for
world models.

---

## Position 12: California Report on Frontier AI Policy (June 2025)

**Co-authored** with Mariano-Florentino Cuéllar and Jennifer Tour
Chayes. Influenced **SB 53.**

Position: evidence-based, nimble regulation focused on
demonstrated marginal harms rather than speculative catastrophic
risk. Pairs with Liang's EU AI Act Code of Practice work in the
same regulatory orbit.

## Disagreements / debates

### vs. Yoshua Bengio — SB 1047
Bengio publicly supported SB 1047 (Fortune, August 15, 2024); Li
opposed it. **Civil disagreement between two respected AI
voices.**

### vs. Gary Marcus — SB 1047 aftermath
Marcus wrote a Substack open letter accusing her of misreading
the bill's kill-switch provisions. **Rare direct public
adversary.** Li did not publicly engage the rebuttal.

### vs. Geoffrey Hinton — x-risk framing
Explicit daylight on existential risk. Both have appeared in
conversation at Stanford HAI civilly. Her Paris 2025 "science
not science fiction" line is her most pointed shot at the
x-risk ecosystem.

### vs. Concentrated compute / tech oligopoly
Ongoing critique of AI being captured by a handful of frontier
labs. NAIRR and AI4ALL are her constructive counter-moves, not
just rhetoric.

### Not in conflict with
- **Elon Musk / xAI** — operates outside his orbit; no surfaced
  direct exchange
- **AI accelerationists (e/acc)** — not a direct adversary; her
  stance is "pragmatic optimist" which puts her sideways to both
  doomers and pure accelerationists

## Predictions / bets

1. **Spatial intelligence is the defining AI challenge of the
   next decade.** LLMs alone will not get there.
2. **3D as the universal interface for spatial systems** — the
   way text became universal interface for software
3. **Robotics breakthroughs are mid-term** — preceded by
   creative-tool adoption (Marble), followed by scientific-
   research / healthcare / education transformation
4. **Open-source + public-sector AI capacity is existentially
   important** — if frontier compute stays in 3-4 labs, the
   ecosystem collapses
5. **Near-term-harms policy outperforms x-risk policy** over 5-
   10-year horizon
6. **Ambient intelligence becomes standard of care** in
   hospitals (PAC research program)
7. **U.S. AI leadership is contingent on immigration + public
   research funding** — not on private compute alone
8. **Hybrid generative stacks win** over pure end-to-end video
   for world models
