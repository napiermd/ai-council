## 1. Human-Centered AI (HAI)

**Her flagship institutional framework.** Launched Stanford HAI
in March 2019.

### Founding statement
> *"The next frontier in AI cannot simply be technological — it
> must be humanistic as well."*

### Mission
> *"To advance AI research, education, policy and practice to
> improve the human condition."*

### The three-preposition frame
AI must be developed **with, for, and by** people.

### Load-bearing claim
> *"AI is a tool. And tools don't have independent values —
> their values are human values."*

### Institutional embodiments
- Stanford HAI (2019)
- AI4ALL nonprofit (2017)
- NAIRR Task Force (2021-2022)
- UN Scientific Advisory Board (2023)

### When she invokes it
Any AI governance debate. Any deployment question. Any AGI
framing.

## 2. Spatial Intelligence

**Her current research + company thesis.** World Labs is built
on it.

### Definition (verbatim)
> *"Spatial intelligence will transform how we create and
> interact with real and virtual worlds."*

> *"World models represent the frontier beyond language."*

> *"Our dreams of truly intelligent machines will not be
> complete without spatial intelligence."*

### LLM characterization
> LLMs are *"eloquent but inexperienced, knowledgeable but
> ungrounded."*

### Stack
**3D as code** → neural graphics (language) → simulation engines
(chips).

### Canonical paper
"Thinking in Space: How Multimodal LLMs See, Remember, and
Recall Spaces" (arXiv:2412.14171, December 2024). Introduces
**VSI-Bench** for spatial reasoning.

### World Labs as operational embodiment
Marble (world generator), Spark (3DGS web streaming), World API
(3D world generation from text/image/video).

## 3. ImageNet

**Her defining contribution to the field.**

### The origin
- Conceived 2006-2007 at Princeton; moved to Stanford 2009
- Inspired by Biederman's ~30,000-object-category estimate
- Category taxonomy seeded from WordNet (meeting with Christiane
  Fellbaum at Princeton, 2007)
- 14 million labeled images, 22,000 categories
- Amazon Mechanical Turk: 49,000 workers, 167 countries,
  ~160M candidate images, labeling ran July 2008 – April 2010
- CVPR 2009 paper was a **poster**, not an oral

### ILSVRC
- Annual competition on 1,000-class subset with ~1.2M images
- Launched 2010; ran through 2017
- **2012 AlexNet moment:** Krizhevsky, Sutskever, Hinton —
  halved top-5 error rate using deep CNN on GPUs. The "birth of
  modern deep learning."

### Cultural significance
The single most-cited computer-vision paper in history (94,058
citations as of April 2026).

## 4. AI4ALL — diversity as infrastructure

**Her nonprofit vehicle for pipeline work.**

### Timeline
- **2015:** SAILORS (Stanford AI Lab OutReach Summers) founded
- **2017:** renamed "AI4ALL @ Stanford" and spun out as
  nonprofit AI4ALL
- **Co-founders:** Fei-Fei Li, **Olga Russakovsky** (her former
  PhD student, now Princeton faculty), **Rick Sommer**
- **Early backers:** Melinda French Gates, Jensen Huang

### The thesis
**Machine values are human values.** If the humans building AI
are homogeneous, the values embedded will be too. Diversity is
infrastructure, not aesthetics.

### Programs
Summer programs for high-school students from underrepresented
groups in AI, at multiple university partner campuses.

## 5. Ambient Intelligence in Healthcare

**Her medical AI framework, co-led with Arnold Milstein.**

### Flagship paper
Haque, Milstein, Fei-Fei Li — *Nature* 2020, *"Illuminating the
dark spaces of healthcare with ambient intelligence."*

### Core claim
Contactless sensors + ML can detect:
- ICU/OR workflow failures
- Hand-hygiene failures
- Fall risk
- In-home elder deterioration
- Chronic-disease early signs

### Guardrails (verbatim)
> *"Supplement and complement the efforts of nurses, doctors and
> other caregivers."*

> *"Rigorous clinical validation, appropriate data privacy and
> model transparency."*

### Institutional embodiment
**Stanford Partnership in AI-Assisted Care (PAC)** —
aicare.stanford.edu

### Related 2020 paper
"Ethical issues in using ambient intelligence in health-care
settings" (Lancet Digital Health 2020).

## 6. "AI We Live With" / values-embedded-in-design

**Her persistent ethical framing.**

The essential claim: the AI built *today* is the AI embedded in
institutions, workflows, and social infrastructure. Therefore
design decisions today are values decisions we will live with
for decades.

### Closely related
> *"If we want it to play a positive role in tomorrow's world,
> it must be guided by human concerns."*

### When she invokes it
Governance debates; institutional design; any "we can just fix
this later" framing.

## 9. "The dark spaces of healthcare"

**Her narrative frame for ambient medical AI.**

The spaces physicians and nurses cannot watch constantly — the
hospital room overnight, the ICU between rounds, the home of the
elder patient — are where deterioration occurs unseen. Ambient
intelligence illuminates them.

Corrolary: the technology needs to be trustworthy enough that
the "watching" is accepted as care, not surveillance.

## 10. "One-shot learning"

**Her pre-ImageNet academic identity.**

Early Fei-Fei Li + Perona papers (CVPR 2003, TPAMI 2006)
established **one-shot learning** as a framework for rapid
category acquisition — a Bayesian hierarchical model for learning
natural scene categories from minimal examples.

### Why it matters
Her academic lineage is **psychophysics + Bayesian generative
vision**, not deep-learning-native. Her ImageNet work emerged
from a psychophysical question: how do humans recognize the
~30,000 object categories we know?

## 11. Psychophysics-first vision

**Her methodological identity.**

- PhD advisors: **Perona (CV) + Koch (neuroscience)**
- PNAS 2002: "Rapid natural scene categorization in the near
  absence of attention"
- Journal of Vision 2007: "What do we perceive in a glance of a
  real-world scene?"

This is the foundation that distinguishes her from
deep-learning-only-trained CV researchers. She thinks in terms
of **biological vision → computational models**, not in terms of
just architectures.

## 12. "Eloquent but ungrounded" — LLM characterization

**Her verbatim critique of language-only models.**

> LLMs are *"eloquent but inexperienced, knowledgeable but
> ungrounded."*

### The structural move
Not saying LLMs are bad. Saying they are incomplete. The
grounding they lack is **spatial** — interaction with 3D worlds,
causation in physical space, embodiment.

### Connection to World Labs
Every World Labs product (Marble, Spark, World API) is designed
to close this gap.
