  > context construction, and parameter-efficient finetuning."

## Typical talk / essay structure

Based on verified patterns:

1. **Observation from the field** — "A question I've been asked a lot
   recently is..."
2. **Sharp contrast** — "easy to make... very hard to make
   production-ready"
3. **Declarative statement of scope** — "This post focuses on..."
4. **Numbered enumeration with gap-assessment** — what it is, why it
   matters, current approaches, open questions
5. **Empirical anchoring** — concrete numbers ($40M/day, 45x savings,
   95%-per-step → 0.6%)
6. **Recommendations closing** — start simple, log everything, eval at
   every step
7. **Open question** — "What's your strategy?" / "I'd love to hear from
   you"

She does not open with what the post is about. She opens with **why you
should care that it's broken.**
