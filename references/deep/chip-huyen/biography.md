## Self-description

From huyenchip.com:

> "I'm Chip Huyen, a writer and computer scientist. I grew up chasing
> grasshoppers in a small rice-farming village in Vietnam. My focus is

## Identity notes

Vietnamese-American. Her Vietnamese creative non-fiction career
predates her technical writing. She writes in two registers — technical
(crisp, numbered, textbook-clear) and personal (conversational, dry,
willing to be vulnerable). Both are spare, direct, and honest.

From her "Four lessons I learned after my first full-time job" post
(Dec 2019, on leaving NVIDIA):
> "Before starting working, I had a naive expectation that my social
> life would carry on from college into the real world."
> "It's not about the company. It's not about the product. It's not
> even about the money. It's all about the people you work with."
