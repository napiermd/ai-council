## Books

### AI Engineering: Building Applications with Foundation Models (O'Reilly, 2025)
- **Publisher:** O'Reilly Media, January 2025
- **Companion repo:** https://github.com/chiphuyen/aie-book
- **O'Reilly link:** https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/ai-engineering/9781098166298/
- **Distinction:** *"The most-read book on the O'Reilly platform since
  its release."* (her books page, 2025)
- **Translations in progress:** Chinese, French, Japanese, Korean,
  Polish, Russian.

### Table of Contents (from aie-book repo)

1. **Introduction to Building AI Applications with Foundation Models**
   — The Rise of AI Engineering, use cases, planning, the AI
   Engineering Stack (3 layers; AI vs ML Engineering; AI vs full-stack)
2. **Understanding Foundation Models** — training data, modeling,
   post-training (SFT, preference finetuning), sampling
3. **Evaluation Methodology** — language modeling metrics, exact
   evaluation, AI-as-judge, comparative evaluation
4. **Evaluate AI Systems** — criteria, model selection, evaluation
   pipeline (3 steps)
5. **Prompt Engineering** — in-context learning, best practices,
   defensive prompt engineering
6. **RAG and Agents** — retrieval algorithms, agents (tools, planning,
   failure modes), memory
7. **Finetuning** — when (and when not), memory bottlenecks, PEFT
8. **Dataset Engineering** — curation, augmentation/synthesis,
   processing
9. **Inference Optimization** — metrics, accelerators, model- and
   service-level optimization
10. **AI Engineering Architecture and User Feedback** — the 5-step
    architecture, monitoring, pipeline orchestration, user feedback

Notable structural choice: **evaluation before prompting, RAG, or
finetuning.** Two full chapters on eval.

### Designing Machine Learning Systems (O'Reilly, 2022)
- **Publisher:** O'Reilly Media, 2022
- **Companion repo:** https://github.com/chiphuyen/dmls-book
- **Status:** Amazon #1 bestseller in AI. Translated into 10+ languages.
- **Genesis:** expansion of her Stanford CS 329S lecture notes.
- **Author's framing:**
  > "a holistic approach to designing ML systems" examining "data
  > processing, feature selection, and model retraining" in the context
  > of "how it can help your system as a whole achieve its objectives."
- **Readability claim:**
  > "Storytelling techniques from creative writing make technical
  > concepts more accessible."
