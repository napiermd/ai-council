# Positions: Aidan Gomez

> Strong public positions on enterprise AI, AGI hype, sovereignty,
> compute economics, and small/specialized models. Each item
> sourced to a verifiable tweet, podcast, interview, or paper.

---

## His signature line — "The actual AI revolution is prosaic"

**Source:** multiple 2025 press summaries; consistent with
podcast statements.

> *"Everyone's obsessed with AGI timelines and whether AI will be
> sentient. But the actual AI revolution happening right now is
> much more prosaic — companies automating document review,
> customer support routing, code documentation."*

This is the anchor framing for all Cohere positioning. Every
other position is a specification of this premise.

## Position 1: Enterprise-first, not consumer

**Source:** Upstarts Media (January 2025); Linkedin essay "Where
Enterprise AI Is Headed" (December 5, 2024).

> *"We don't do these blockbuster launch event type things. We
> just want to quietly do good work, build really good software,
> and focus on becoming an essential partner to our customers."*
— Upstarts Media, January 2025

> *"I looked at the enterprise software landscape and realized
> no one was building foundation models optimized for business
> use cases."*
— attributed

> *"People are used to working with systems that are half blind,
> or have an arm tied behind their back."*
— Upstarts Media, January 2025

Cohere explicitly **does not compete with ChatGPT on consumer
AI.** This is a strategic identity, not a tactical choice.

## Position 4: Compute-maximalism is the "dumbest" path

**Source:** BetaKit via Nick Frosst interview; Gomez's parallel
framing.

> *"More compute is the most trustworthy way to improve AI
> models, it's also the dumbest."*

### Empirical backing — Cohere's small-model strategy
- **Tiny Aya** (Feb 17, 2026): 3.35B params, single cluster of
  64 H100s, 70+ languages (India AI Impact Summit)
- **Transcribe** (Mar 26, 2026): 2B params, consumer-GPU
  deployable, #1 on Hugging Face Open ASR leaderboard (5.42 WER)
- **Command A** (March 13, 2025): 111B params, 256K context, runs
  on 2 GPUs

### Position
Efficient, specialized enterprise models beat frontier-generalist
scale for enterprise deployment.

foundation-model vendor. The Germany / Aleph Alpha merger is
the largest test.

## Position 13: Hybrid open / closed licensing

**Source:** Cohere Labs (Aya) vs Cohere (Command) licensing.

### Philosophy
- **Aya (Cohere Labs):** open weights, research-only license (CC
  non-commercial)
- **Command (Cohere commercial):** weights on Hugging Face under
  CC-BY-NC — not fully commercial open-source
- **Transcribe (March 2026):** open source ASR, free API,
  positioned for research + self-hosting

### Stated position
> *"We have strongly supported the wider academic AI research
> community from the start, and that's why we set up our
> non-profit research lab Cohere For AI."*

Different from Meta (fully open-weight), Anthropic (fully
closed), and Mistral (mixed commercial open).
