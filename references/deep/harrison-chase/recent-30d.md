## Chase-authored blog posts (in-window)

### April 11, 2026 — "Your harness, your memory"
- 7 min read
- **Flagship 2026 position piece.** Names Claude Managed Agents
  and OpenAI Responses API as "worst" tier of closed-harness
  lock-in
- Top quotes (verbatim):
  - *"Agent harnesses are not going away."*
  - *"Managing context, and therefore memory, is a core capability
    and responsibility of the agent harness."*
  - *"If you use a closed harness, especially if it's behind an
    API, you don't own your memory."*
  - *"This is incredibly alarming — it means that memory will
    become locked into a single platform, a single model."*
  - *"Without memory, your agents are easily replicable by anyone
    who has access to the same tools. With memory, you build up a
    proprietary dataset."*

### April 5, 2026 — "Continual learning for AI agents"
- 4 min read
- **Three-layer thesis:**
  *"Learning can happen at three distinct layers: the model, the
  harness, and the context."*
- *"All of these flows are powered by traces — the full execution
  path of what an agent did."*
- Positions LangSmith traces as the substrate for self-improving
  harnesses — the data flywheel claim
