### Define Ventures — advisor-announcement essay (August 13, 2025)
- **Richest single source for his founder-facing voice.**
- **URL:** https://www.definevc.com/insights/introducing-dr-nigam-shah-chief-data-scientist-at-stanford-health-care-as-an-advisor-to-define-ventures
- **Direct quotes:**
  > "The journey of taking research to a product belongs in a company."
  > "Companies are a logical next step in the evolution of an idea and

### Stanford Medicine Insights — "Evaluating AI in context: Which LLM is best for real health care needs?" (April 8, 2025)
- **URL:** https://med.stanford.edu/news/insights/2025/04/ai-artificial-intelligence-evaluation-algorithm.html
- **Direct quotes:**
  > "It's not enough for a large language model to simply answer medical
  > test questions accurately."
  > "That type of evaluation doesn't tell us anything about what matters."
  > "About 95% of LLM evaluations that are reported in the literature
  > are not done using electronic health record data — and that context
  > is really important."
  > "MedHELM doesn't approve models or guarantee a specific level of
  > accuracy — it's a tool that helps inform users on the best place to
  > start developing a model specific to their needs."

### Stanford Medicine inaugural-CDS announcement (March 2022)
- **URL:** https://med.stanford.edu/news/all-news/2022/03/nigam-shah-inaugural-chief-data-scientist-stanford-health-care.html
- **Direct quotes:**
  > "There's been a lot of excitement around using AI, algorithms and
  > data to improve health care. But broadly speaking, there's been more
  > talk about using AI than actual implementation of AI in health care
  > delivery. Now, it's time to change that."
  > "It's all about improving patient care. I want patients to say,
  > 'This was the most proactive care I've ever had,' or 'Scheduling was
  > a breeze,' or 'My surgery ran late, but thank goodness my wife was
  > informed that she should come an hour later.' These things aren't
  > necessarily going to be the basis for big flashy papers, but that's
  > okay."
  > "We need to be cognizant of equity and fairness when considering
  > adopting AI-guided decision making and be open to the possibility
  > that there will be situations in which we should not be using AI."
  > "Building and integrating an algorithm into any workflow will have
  > ripple effects that are beyond just what the algorithm does. So
  > we're thinking about AI integration as an overall delivery science.
  > It's not just the algorithms we need to consider; the algorithms
  > and clinicians must work as partners."
  > "What if we could do that — and more — for every single patient?
  > Broadly speaking, part of this is looking at how AI can support
  > personalization of health care at a massive scale. The point is to
  > bring AI into clinical use safely, ethically and cost effectively,
  > writ large."

### TechTarget — "AI-native EHRs: a new era or just a new label?" (November 17, 2025)
- **URL:** https://www.techtarget.com/searchhealthit/feature/AI-native-EHRs-A-new-era-or-just-a-new-label
- **Direct quotes:**
  > "It's a buzzword for sure. People need buzzwords. But if an EHR
  > company started after this AI revolution began, it can be AI native
  > — legitimately."
  > "You can only be a native if you began when the thing was around.
  > If a company has already existed for 20 years and is now adding AI

## Social media voice

### LinkedIn (`/in/nigam`) — primary long-form channel
Heavier posting cadence than X. Posts tend to be issue-driven, heavy on
citations, conversational-didactic. Typical length: 2–6 paragraphs plus
hashtags (#responsibleai, #healthcareai, #llm).

- **External-validation post (late 2023):** weather-forecasting
  analogy; argues external validation neither guarantees generalizability
  nor equates to clinical usefulness.
- **"Five questions to ask before using LLMs" (2023):** full five-question
  framework reproduced in `frameworks.md`.
- **RAIL quarter-in-review (late 2023):** self-describes the work as
  "at the cutting, bleeding, agonizing and fun edge."
- **EHRSHOT release post (2024):** "Honest evaluation of clinical
  benefit requires benchmarks that contain longitudinal patient data.
  Data sharing for such evaluation is scant even though risks are shown
  to be low."
- **Systematic-review post (2024):** "Current evaluations of LLMs in
  healthcare are sub-optimal (95% do not use real patient records!)"

### X / Twitter (`@drnigam`)
Lower posting volume than LinkedIn. Primarily retweets/amplifies
Stanford HAI, Stanford DBDS, CHAI, Shah Lab, NEJM AI. Reacts to hype
with understatement, not dunking. Recurring quote vehicles: Brittany
Trang (STAT health-tech reporter), STAT News.
