## His typical talk structure

- **Open with a factual statement**, not a hook.
- **Show the terminal** — his talks are full of live demos and
  screenshots.
- **Name the thing** if he's coining. Name everything if he's listing.
- **Credit sources by name** — every time.
- **Close on a question** or a "here's the repo."

## Signature talk quotes

### "There has never been a better time to learn to program"
(AI Engineer Summit closing keynote, Oct 2023)

### On accountability
> "Only we as humans can attach our credibility to the things that we
> produce."
> — AI Engineer World's Fair, Jun 2024

### On security
> "If you don't [understand prompt injection], you'll make the same
> mistake that these six different well resourced teams made."
> — AI Engineer World's Fair, Jun 2024

### On pelicans
> "I'm running this against text output LLMs. They shouldn't be able
> to draw anything at all. But they can generate code... and SVG is
> code."

> "Pelicans can't ride bicycles. They're the wrong shape!"
> — AI Engineer World's Fair, Jun 2025

### On LLMs as weird interns
> "If a friend could read a Wikipedia page and answer my question, the
> LLM will too. But if it's something Wikipedia probably won't cover,
> it's less likely the LLM will answer it."
> — Software Misadventures podcast, Sep 2024

### On shipping
> "Half the world runs on Excel spreadsheets with no unit tests... and
> society functions. Maybe goop is the way forward."
> — Software Misadventures, Sep 2024

### On progress (Latent Space, Jan 2025)
> "Everything's got really good and fast and cheap. The good models
> got so much cheaper, faster, multimodal."

> "I ran another GPT-4 model on my laptop this morning... it's a 14
> gigabyte download and I can run it on MacBook Pro."

### On gullibility (Latent Space, Jan 2025)
> "LLMs believe anything you tell them... systems attempting meaningful
> decisions will hit the same roadblock."

> "ChatGPT can never stake its reputation on telling you something
> useful because that means nothing."

### On LLMs breaking the internet (Changelog #534, Apr 2023)
> "[LLMs] can be flawed, and lying, and have all of these problems...
> and it can also be a massive productivity boost."
