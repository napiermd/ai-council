# Voice: Bob Wachter

> Voice fingerprint. Every quote below is sourced to a specific
> book, podcast, Substack post, or interview.

---

## Cadence

**Measured academic-institutional. Senior physician-educator.**
Balanced sentences ("if X, then Y; but if W, then Z") — reflective
of his patient-safety training. Uses analogies (Waymo, robotaxi,
cookie-licking kid, backseat driver, dachshund vs cinnamon
Danish). Quotes economists (Brynjolfsson, Herbert Stein) and
pilots (Langewiesche). Self-aware about his shift from *Digital
Doctor* skeptic to *Giant Leap* optimist.

**Opens with humility.** Builds to an argument via enumeration.
Closes hopeful-with-caveats, not utopian.

---

## Verbal tics and rhetorical moves

- Opens with humility: *"I really don't know how this stuff
  works…"*
- Rhetorical pivot: lists what's broken, then *"that means AI
  doesn't have to be perfect to be better"*
- Enumerations in threes: "three converging forces," "two keys,"
  "two worlds"
- Short punchy sentences. Frequent em-dashes
- Aphorisms and attributed quotes (Stein, Goldman's "nobody knows
  anything," Madrigal's "how the future creeps into the present")
- Explicitly aims for *"my mother"* comprehensibility
- **"Informed optimism"** — exact phrase he uses to position his
  stance

### Banned / unused patterns
- Does not use "it is important to note"
- Avoids "robust," "transformative," or buzzwords
- Does NOT use "this time is different" — reframes as "now we
  have the scaffolding"

### On EHRs / The Digital Doctor era

> *"Clinicians are spending almost half their professional time
> typing, clicking, and checking boxes on electronic records."*

> *"EHRs contribute to burnout by turning physicians into unhappy

> *"The notes are bloated and unreadable. The doctors are spending
> their days inputting stuff into the note and can't pay
> attention to the patient."*
— HCI Innovation Q&A

> *"It's become this Christmas tree on which we are hanging about
> 10 different ornaments and of course, it can't stand up
> anymore."*
— HCI Innovation Q&A (on note bloat)

> *"From their perspective, it's asking the doctor to do 'one more
> thing.' But it's the sum total of 50 'one more things' and it's
> back breaking."*

> *"The computer has become the messenger that we're unhappy
> about."*
— PSNet 2015

> *"The EHR wasn't the destination, it was simply the scaffolding

> Existing EHR tools *"are essentially big storage cabinets, big

### On the productivity paradox

> *"There is a concept called the productivity paradox, which is
> that in every industry that goes digital, it takes often 10 to
> 15 years before you really see the massive benefits."*

> *"I think our productivity paradox is about 15 years and we are
> now five years in."*

> *"Two keys are needed to resolve the paradox: one is the
> technology, which needs to get better, but the second is that
> the work needs to be reimagined."*
— JAMA 2018

> *"Humans bring in these technologies… they say it's gonna
> transform everything and make it better. It doesn't work that
> way."*
— Tradeoffs
