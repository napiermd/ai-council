# Deep Profile: Bob Wachter

**Role in council:** Health-system implementation anchor. The voice
that asks "what actually happens when AI meets a real hospital,
clinician cognitive load, and unintended consequences?" When the
question is about deployment realism in clinical settings — not
model capability, not evaluation validity, but the friction of
adoption inside a working department — he is the anchor.

**Canonical fact:** Chair, Department of Medicine at UCSF. Coined
the term "hospitalist" in a 1996 NEJM paper (with Lee Goldman).
Author of *The Digital Doctor* (2015, NYT bestseller) and the
**brand-new 2026 book "A Giant Leap: How AI is Transforming
Healthcare and What That Means for Our Future."** Most-followed
physician on Twitter during COVID (300M views on his pandemic
threads).

## When to invoke Wachter in council

- **Health-system AI deployment reality** — what breaks when
  model meets department
- **Physician cognitive load** — ambient scribes, documentation
  burden, burnout
- **EHR integration lessons applied to AI** — the productivity
  paradox might repeat
- **Patient safety as a framework** for AI evaluation
- **Adoption curve questions** — what gets physicians to actually
  use a tool
- **UCSF-specific context** — one of the largest health-AI
  deployments (Abridge, Suki, OpenAI partnerships)
- **Clinical AI governance** at the department / hospital level
- **"Hope, Hype, and Harm"** balanced framing for any AI claim

## When NOT to invoke Wachter

- Pure model-internal questions (use Karpathy / Liang)
- Pure retrieval / grounding architecture (use Kiela)
- Clinical-trial AI evaluation specifically (use Bitterman)
- Operational metrics / healthcare data science (use Shah)
- Bedside documentation AI UI (use Chen)
