# Biography: Bob Wachter

> Robert M. Wachter, MD. Born 1957, New York City. Professor and
> 9th Chair of the Department of Medicine at UCSF. Coined the
> term "hospitalist" in a 1996 NEJM paper with Lee Goldman.
> Author of *The Digital Doctor* (2015) and *A Giant Leap* (2026).

## Career

### Joined UCSF faculty — 1990
After completing Stanford RWJ Clinical Scholars.

### Hospitalist movement origin — 1996
- **August 15, 1996:** *"The Emerging Role of 'Hospitalists' in
  the American Health Care System"* — Wachter RM, Goldman L.
  NEJM 335(7):514–517. DOI: 10.1056/NEJM199608153350713
- **April 1997:** convened the first educational conference
  dedicated to hospital medicine, San Francisco. Drs. Win Whitcomb
  and John Nelson raised the idea of forming a professional
  society
- **1997–1998:** National Association of Inpatient Physicians
  (NAIP) founded — opened with 300 founding hospitalists
- **2000:** elected **first president** of NAIP
- **2003:** NAIP renamed **Society of Hospital Medicine (SHM)**
- **2010:** received **Master of Hospital Medicine (MHM)**
  designation from SHM
- **2016 NEJM follow-up:** *"Zero to 50,000 — The 20th Anniversary
  of the Hospitalist"* (Wachter & Goldman). The field grew from
  0 → ~50,000 practicing hospitalists over 20 years — the
  fastest-growing medical specialty in U.S. history

### UCSF Department of Medicine leadership
- **2015–2016:** Interim Chair (one year)
- **October 6, 2016:** named **9th Chair** of the UCSF Department
  of Medicine. Still serving as Chair as of April 2026
- **Chair appointment quote (verbatim):**
  > *"I see my role as chair as helping to set our strategic
  > direction and — more importantly — creating an environment in
  > which we attract, retain, and support the most accomplished,
  > most interesting, most dynamic, and most diverse people."*

### Endowed chairs
- **2007:** Lynne and Marc Benioff Endowed Chair in Hospital
  Medicine (endowment established)
- **Holly Smith Distinguished Professor in Science and Medicine**
  (date not specified in primary sources)

### ABIM Board chair — 2013–2014
First hospitalist to chair the American Board of Internal
Medicine Board of Directors. Named Chair July 2012 (announced);
served 2013–2014.

### NHS — 2015–2016
Chaired the "blue-ribbon commission advising England's National
Health Service on its digital strategy" — the **"Wachter
Review"**: *"Making IT Work: Harnessing the Power of Health
Information Technology to Improve Care in England"* (September
2016). Still cited as influential in NHS digital policy as of
2023.

### COVID-19 public voice — 2020–2023
One of the most-followed physicians on Twitter during the
pandemic. Per Wikipedia, his tweets were *"viewed over 300 million
times by more than 250,000 followers."* Served as a trusted
pandemic information source for the general public.

### Macy Foundation Board of Directors — 2024–present
Macy convenes AI + medical-education conferences. Wachter's board
role bridges his clinical-AI work with medical-education policy.

### Book arc — the 2015 → 2026 trajectory
- **2015:** *The Digital Doctor* (NYT bestseller) — skeptical
  framing of health IT
- **November 30, 2023:** JAMA paper with Brynjolfsson (one-year
  anniversary of ChatGPT) — qualified optimism
- **February 3, 2026:** *A Giant Leap* (instant national
  bestseller) — bullish-with-caveats

The arc itself is part of his public identity: measured public
figure whose view has evolved with the technology.

## UCSF Division of Hospital Medicine

Wachter was the founding leader. By 2016 the division had grown
to ~65 physicians. It is one of the largest hospital medicine
programs in the country.
