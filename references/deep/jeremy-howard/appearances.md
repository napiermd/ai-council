## Canonical podcasts

### Lex Fridman Podcast #35 — "fast.ai Deep Learning Courses and Research"
- **Date:** August 27, 2019. **Runtime:** ~1h 44m.
- **URL:** https://lexfridman.com/jeremy-howard/
- **Transcript:** https://podscripts.co/podcasts/lex-fridman-podcast/jeremy-howard-fastai-deep-learning-courses-and-research

**Verbatim quotes:**
> "Rather than me picking an area and trying to become good at it
> and building something, I should let people who are already domain
> experts in those areas and who already have the data do it
> themselves."

> "Train lots of models. That's how you learn it... train models,
> print out the inputs to the models, print out the outputs to the
> models, like study, you know, change the inputs a bit, look at how
> the outputs vary."

> "The key differentiator between people that succeed and people that
> fail is tenacity, that seems to be basically the only thing that
> matters."

> "Most of the research in the deep [learning] world is a total waste
> of time... you get just a whole lot of research, which is minor
> advances in stuff that's been very highly studied and has no
> significant practical impact."

> "Transfer learning is this like world-changing thing... but almost
> nobody works on that... it's just not a trendy thing right now."

> "Python's so slow, it's extremely unhackable... from here on down at
> C... your debugger doesn't work in the same way, your profile
> doesn't work in the same way, it's really not very hackable at all."

> "Everything we do is free. We have no revenue sources of any kind.
> It's just a service to the community."

> "We don't need more experts... creating slightly evolutionary
> research in areas that everybody's studying. We need experts at
> using deep learning to diagnose malaria... that's what we need."

> "Being pragmatic and trying to stay away from venture capital money
> as long as possible, preferably forever."

### fast.ai course lectures (the canonical teaching body)
- Practical Deep Learning for Coders v1 (2016) → v5 (2022) → 2024-25
  rebuild
- "From Deep Learning Foundations to Stable Diffusion" (30+ hrs, 2023)
- v5 recorded at University of Queensland
- **URL:** https://course.fast.ai/

**Pedagogical framing (verbatim from course page):**
> "In this course, I start by showing how to use a complete, working,
> very usable, state-of-the-art deep learning network to solve
> real-world problems, using simple, expressive tools."

> "And then we gradually dig deeper and deeper into understanding how
> those tools are made... We always teach through examples. We ensure
> that there is a context and a purpose that you can understand
> intuitively, rather than starting with algebraic symbol
> manipulation."

Prerequisite: *"The only prerequisite is that you know how to code (a
year of experience is enough), preferably in Python, and that you
have at least followed a high school math course."*

## Australian connections

From the **aarora.github.io meetup transcript** (May 15, 2021):

> "The amount of funding of startups in Australia in a year is less
> than the amount of funding of startups in the US in a day."

> "Every time we'd say we want to do `X`, people would say 'Woah,
> okay, that's great. What if you could make an even bigger `X`?'"
> (US) vs *"Why aren't you OK with what's already there? Why build
> something new?"* (AUS)

> "Australian customers are so conservative, that if you want to sell
> something to enterprises in Australia, you have to tell them that
> their competitors have already bought it."

> "Do it in as un-Australian a way as possible: you don't have to
> have Australian investors or customers."

> "The Australian talent pool is just fantastic. But, there's this
> huge difference in opportunity and belief."

**URL:** https://amaarora.github.io/posts/2021-05-15-jh-meetup.html

- Regular Sunrise (Channel 7) morning TV guest.
