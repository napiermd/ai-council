### Core thesis
Treat AI as **"co-intelligence"** — a collaborator to work *with*,
not a tool to wield. LLMs are *"smart but alien"* — neither a
search engine nor a person, but a new kind of partner whose
capability is uneven.

### The four rules (verbatim from the book)

1. **"Always invite AI to the table."** Use it for everything
   ethically permissible; discover the jagged frontier by trying.
2. **"Be the human in the loop."** Stay active — cross-check,
   supervise, question outputs. Don't delegate judgment.
3. **"Treat AI like a person (but tell it what kind of person it
   is)."** Personas improve output quality; prompt it as a role
   (e.g., *"a physicist with a PhD from Caltech"*).
4. **"Assume this is the worst AI you will ever use."** Current
   model = floor, not ceiling. Plan for rapid capability growth.

## The canonical Jagged Frontier paper

### "Navigating the Jagged Technological Frontier: Field Experimental Evidence of the Effects of Artificial Intelligence on Knowledge Worker Productivity and Quality"

- **Working paper:** Harvard Business School Working Paper
  No. 24-013 (September 2023). SSRN 4573321
- **Published version:** *Organization Science* 2025. DOI:
  10.1287/orsc.2025.21838
- **Authors (full):** Fabrizio Dell'Acqua, Edward McFowland III,
  **Ethan R. Mollick**, Hila Lifshitz-Assaf, Katherine C. Kellogg,
  Saran Rajendran, Lisa Krayer, François Candelon, Karim R.
  Lakhani

### Design
Pre-registered field experiment with Boston Consulting Group:
- **N = 758 consultants**
- **Three conditions:**
  (a) no AI
  (b) GPT-4 access
  (c) GPT-4 + prompt-engineering overview

### Key findings (inside-frontier tasks)
- **Speed:** +25.1%
- **Human-rated quality:** +40%+
- **Task completion:** +12.2%

### The "Jagged Frontier" concept (originates here)
AI capability is uneven and non-obvious — it helps on some tasks
and hurts on others of similar apparent difficulty, even within
the same workflow. **Outside-frontier tasks showed degraded
performance** — consultants who trusted AI on a task where it
failed did worse than controls.

### Performance convergence finding
**Bottom-half performers gained more than top performers.** AI
compresses skill distributions by raising the floor faster than
the ceiling. (The commonly-cited "30% vs 90%" framing is
Mollick's popularized version — exact numbers vary by framing.)
