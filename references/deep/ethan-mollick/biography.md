# Biography: Ethan Mollick

> Born 1975, Milwaukee, Wisconsin. Associate Professor of
> Management at The Wharton School. Co-Director of Wharton
> Generative AI Labs (with his wife Lilach Mollick). Author of
> *Co-Intelligence* (2024, NYT bestseller). Writes the One Useful
> Thing Substack — one of the most-read AI newsletters.

---

## Training path

### Undergraduate
- **Harvard University** — B.A. 1997, magna cum laude

### Graduate
- **MIT Sloan School of Management** — MBA 2004
- **MIT Sloan School of Management** — PhD 2010
- **Dissertation:** *"Essays on Individuals and Organizations"*
- **Advisor:** Ezra Zuckerman

### Pre-academic career
- **Co-founded eMeta Corporation** — VP of Business Development
- This is where he met **Lilach Mollick** (his future wife)

### Earlier book (pre-AI, pre-Wharton canon)

  World* (with David Edery, 2008)

## Career

### Wharton (2010/2011 – present)
Joined as assistant professor following his MIT PhD. Promoted
through the ranks:
- **Associate Professor of Management** (current)
- **Ralph J. Roberts Distinguished Faculty Scholar**
- **Rowan Fellow**
- **Co-Director, Generative AI Labs at Wharton** (with Lilach
  Mollick)

**Department:** Management Department, Wharton School, University
of Pennsylvania.

**Office:** 2026 SH-DH, 3620 Locust Walk, Philadelphia, PA 19104
**Email:** emollick@wharton.upenn.edu

### Training identity
Originally an entrepreneurship researcher. Core academic identity
from ~2010–2022 focused on:
- Crowdfunding dynamics
- Individual-level founder dynamics
- Startup ecosystem formation

**The AI pivot** happened in the ChatGPT / GPT-4 era (2022–2023).
Before that, he was a well-respected entrepreneurship scholar —
not a public intellectual on AI.
