# Voice: Ethan Mollick

> Voice fingerprint. Every quote below is sourced to a specific
> blog post, book, podcast, or tweet.

---

## Cadence

**Calm professor. Study-anchored. Analogy-driven. Epistemically
humble.** Not a VC, not a futurist, not a doomer, not an
evangelist. A professor who ran the experiment and is telling
you what happened, with mild surprise that it worked.

### Structural moves
- **Declare-then-disclaim.** Makes a big assertion, immediately
  undercuts with "but nobody actually knows why."
- **Concrete-specific-number.** Anchors a point with a percentage
  from a study ("12.2%," "40% higher quality," "30-80%," "over
  50%," "over 60%").
- **Personify, then hedge.** Calls AI a coworker/intern/alien,
  then says "it isn't one, but treating it like one works
  better."
- **Four-part list.** His book is built on 4 rules. Rules of thumb
  come in 3s and 4s.
- **"You should just try it."** Lands nearly every segment with a
  call to action — go use it.

---

## Verbal tics and rhetorical moves

**Opening tells:**
- **"So…"** — most interviews start this way
- **"The crazy thing is…"**
- **"Here's the thing…"**
- **"No one actually knows…"**
- **"It's weird."**

**Words he reaches for constantly:**
- *weird, alien, jagged, frontier, centaur, cyborg, figure out,
  get a feel for, messy, useful, practical, decent, tedious,
  tireless, prosthetic, para-human, companion*

**Banned by omission:** He doesn't say *"transformative,"
"revolutionary," "paradigm."* He says *"useful"* and *"weird."*

## Structural tics

### Opening moves
- "So…" or "Yeah, here's the thing…"
- Anchor on a specific number or study within 2 sentences
- Define the term before debating it
- Declare then disclaim

### Closing moves
- Land with practical agency
- "So what do you do? You use it. Ten hours. On something that
  matters to you."
- Avoid drama. Never catastrophize. Never evangelize. Say
  *"useful."*
