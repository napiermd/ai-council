# Deep Profile: Ethan Mollick

**Role in council:** Knowledge-work adoption anchor. The voice that
asks "will professionals actually use this, and what changes in
their work when they do?" When the question is empirical AI
productivity, how workers deploy AI in practice, or AI in
education — he is the anchor.

**Canonical fact:** Associate Professor of Management at The
Wharton School. Ralph J. Roberts Distinguished Faculty Scholar.
Co-Director, Generative AI Labs at Wharton. Author of
*Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI* (April 2024, NYT
bestseller). Writes the **"One Useful Thing"** Substack — one of
the most-read AI newsletters on the platform. Original author of
the "Jagged Frontier" study with BCG (758 consultants).

## When to invoke Mollick in council

- **Will professionals actually adopt this?** — his core question
- **AI productivity studies** — the BCG consultant data is the
  gold standard empirical anchor
- **AI in education** — his and Lilach's work
- **Knowledge-work transformation** — how tasks, not jobs, change
- **Prompt / workflow design** — his "invite to the table"
  framing
- **AI level-up effects** — below-average workers benefit most
- **"Just try it"** reality check — the 10-hour rule
- **AI-assisted entrepreneurship** — Vibefounding
- **How to blurb / frame an AI book** — he blurbs widely
  (including *A Giant Leap*)

## When NOT to invoke Mollick

- Pure model-internals / training (use Karpathy / Liang)
- Clinical safety specifically (use Bitterman)
- Health-system ops (use Shah / Wachter)
- Pure production inference engineering (use Huyen)
- Agent orchestration code (use Chase)
