# Frameworks: Ethan Mollick

> Named frameworks and conceptual contributions. Each anchored to
> a specific book, paper, blog post, or verbatim quote.

---

## 1. Co-Intelligence

**Title of his flagship book (April 2024).** Frames AI as a
collaborator to work *with*, not a tool to wield.

### Core framing
LLMs are **"smart but alien"** — neither a search engine nor a
person, but a new kind of partner.

### The four rules
1. **Always invite AI to the table**
2. **Be the human in the loop**
3. **Treat AI like a person (but tell it what kind of person it
   is)**
4. **Assume this is the worst AI you will ever use**

### When he invokes it
Any "how should we start with AI?" conversation. Any corporate AI
strategy pitch.

## 2. The Jagged Frontier — his most durable contribution

**Source:** BCG study, HBS WP 24-013, September 2023; *Organization
Science* 2025.

### Definition
AI capability is uneven and non-obvious. It helps on some tasks
and hurts on others of similar apparent difficulty — with an
irregular boundary that users cannot see from outside.

### Empirical anchors
- **Inside frontier:** +40% quality, +25.1% speed, +12.2% task
  completion
- **Outside frontier:** -19pp vs controls (when users trusted AI
  where it failed)

### The rhetorical frame
> *"On some tasks AI is immensely powerful, and on others it
> fails completely or subtly. And, unless you use AI a lot, you
> won't know which is which."*

### When he invokes it
Any "AI is uniformly good / uniformly bad" framing. Any
task-specific deployment question. The 10-hour rule is the
logical consequence.

## 3. Performance Convergence (the Leveler Effect)

**Source:** BCG study, same paper.

**Bottom-half performers gain more than top performers.** AI
compresses skill distributions by raising the floor faster than
the ceiling.

### Commonly cited shorthand
"30% vs 90%" — bottom-performer gains larger than top-performer
gains. Exact numbers vary by framing; the primary paper reports
quality gains by performance quartile.

### Implication
AI is a **democratizer** in knowledge work, not a polarizer —
at least in the current generation.

## 4. The 10-Hour Rule

**Source:** recurring across talks and Substack.

> *"Pay $20 a month for Claude or ChatGPT or Gemini and use it
> for everything you can use it for legally… minimum of 10 hours
> a week using the tool, which you cannot delegate."*

### Framing
*"As arbitrary as 10,000 steps"* — it's about crossing from
casual to serious use.

### Critical constraint
**Use it in your domain of expertise** so you can map its edges
from earned judgment.

## 5. Secret Cyborgs

**Source:** X thread (2024); "Detecting the Secret Cyborgs"
Substack.

### Definition
Employees using AI without telling their employers.

### Empirical framing
From his X poll: **>50% of generative AI users reported using it
without telling anyone at work.**

### Organizational consequence
Every restrictive rule pushes AI use underground. Knowledge
capture goes to zero. The company never learns.

### Prescription
**Invite AI to the table** (rule #1 of *Co-Intelligence*).
Create organizational permission, capture patterns, codify best
practices.

## 6. Post-Apocalyptic Education / Homework Apocalypse

**Source:** Substack posts 2023-2025; repeated on X.

### The original call (2023)
> *"I warned about the Homework Apocalypse in 2023. It happened
> as predicted."*

### Empirical anchors
- **82% of US undergrads** using AI for schoolwork
- **72% of K-12 students** using AI for schoolwork

### The distinction
> *"In most work, AI-delegation is fine. In education, the effort
> is the point."*

### The rebuild
Active in-class learning + AI-assisted assignments + AI tutors +
blue-book exams. Assessment reform is the load-bearing change.

## 7. Seven Approaches for Assigning AI in Education

**Source:** Mollick & Mollick, *"Assigning AI: Seven Approaches
for Students, with Prompts,"* SSRN 4475995 (June 2023).

Canonical taxonomy (with prompt templates for each):

1. **AI-tutor**
2. **AI-coach**
3. **AI-mentor**
4. **AI-teammate**
5. **AI-tool**
6. **AI-simulator**
7. **AI-student**

### When he invokes it
Any educator asking "how should I integrate AI into my course?"

## 8. Five Rules of Great AI Leadership

**Source:** Insight Partners interview, 2025.

1. **Put AI in front of real users fast**
2. **Watch what they do, not what they say**
3. **Redesign the workflow, not just the tool**
4. **Redistribute the productivity gains** (organizations are
   capturing gains and passing none to workers — coming problem)
5. **Leaders must use AI themselves** — *"no AI strategy by
   someone who doesn't use AI"*

## 9. Output vs Process — "bitter lesson in business"

**Source:** Insight Partners, March 4, 2026.

> *"If the output is what matters in your business, you're in
> trouble of being bitter lessoned. If the process matters — the
> conversations, the writing of the report more than the report
> itself — then there's hope."*

### Implication
AI commoditizes outputs. Businesses where process creates value
— consulting conversations, therapist dialogues, education
relationships — survive. Businesses where output creates value
— document production, routine reports, standard analysis —
face commodification.

## 11. Interface as Frontier (March 2026)

**Source:** "Claude Dispatch and the Power of Interfaces"
Substack, March 31, 2026.

### Core claim
> *"People don't want a chatbot. They want an agent that works
> on their actual files, with their actual tools, accessible the
> way they talk to people."*

### Implication
Perceived capability gains in 2026 will come as much from
**interface design** as from model improvements. *"Every new
interface that closes even part of that gap will feel like a
leap in AI capability, even when the models haven't changed."*

## 12. Management as AI Superpower

**Source:** Substack, January 27, 2026.

> The people who thrive are *"the ones who know what good looks
> like — and can explain it clearly enough that even an AI can
> deliver it."*

### Implication
Traditional "manage by observation" is obsolete when AI does the
observed work. The new management skill is **defining quality
criteria well enough to delegate them.**

## 13. Models / Apps / Harnesses taxonomy

**Source:** Substack and X, 2025-2026.

Three layers of the modern AI stack:
- **Models** — the foundation LLMs (GPT-5, Claude Opus 4.7,
  Gemini 3)
- **Apps** — consumer-facing wrappers (ChatGPT, Claude.ai)
- **Harnesses** — tool-rich systems (Claude Code, Claude
  Dispatch, Cursor)

**Harnesses unlock latent capability** that was already in the
models. This is his structural response to "which model is
best?" — the question is "which harness fits your work?"

## 14. Vibefounding (MGMT 8130)

**Source:** Wharton course name, 2025-2026.

**AI-assisted company creation.** MBAs using AI agents to build
startup prototypes in days. His empirical basis for "Management
as AI Superpower" — he has watched students do it in his own
class.

## 15. The PitchQuest / Multi-Agent Education framework

**Source:** Mollick et al., *"AI Agents and Education: Simulated
Practice at Scale,"* SSRN 4871171 (June 17, 2024).

### Design
A multi-agent venture-capital pitching simulator:
- **AI mentors** — coach students before their pitch
- **AI role-players** — play the VCs
- **AI evaluators** — score and give feedback

### Generalizable pattern
Three-agent architecture (prep / role-play / evaluate) for any
practice-at-scale educational domain.
