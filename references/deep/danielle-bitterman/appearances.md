## Typical talk structure

1. **Enthusiasm calibration** — acknowledge the excitement.
2. **However / evaluation gap** — pivot to what's missing.
3. **Specific clinical example** — oncology-grounded.
4. **Quantitative failure finding** — rates from her papers.
5. **Methodological prescription** — TRIPOD-LLM, clinical-grade
   evaluation.
6. **Community action item** — "As a community, we need to..."

---

## Signature positions reinforced in talks

- **Clinical-grade evaluation as methodology**
- **Accuracy ≠ safety** — "mixing bad with good" failure mode
- **Human-in-the-loop is necessary but not sufficient**
- **Pretraining is a safety surface** (Cross-Care + RABBITS)
- **Harmlessness > helpfulness in clinical contexts**
