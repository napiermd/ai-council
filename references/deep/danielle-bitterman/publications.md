### 2. ChatGPT for Cancer Treatment Information (JAMA Oncology 2023)
**Chen S, Kann BH, Foote MB, Aerts HJWL, Savova GK, Mak RH, Bitterman
DS.** "Use of Artificial Intelligence Chatbots for Cancer Treatment
Information." *JAMA Oncology* 9(10):1459-1462, 2023.
DOI: 10.1001/jamaoncol.2023.2954.
**~263 citations. Senior author.**
PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10450584/

**Findings:**
- 98% (102/104) of prompts generated ≥1 recommendation
- **Only 63.5% (66/104) fully NCCN-concordant**
- **34.3% (35/102) recommended at least one non-concordant treatment
  alongside correct options** — "hide the bad inside the good"
- **12.5% (13/104) contained hallucinated treatments** (not found in
  any guideline)
- Inter-rater complete agreement: 61.9%

### 4. LLM for Patient Messages (Lancet Digital Health 2024)
**Chen S, Guevara M, Moningi S, Hoebers F, Elhalawani H, Kann BH,
Chipidza FE, Leeman J, Aerts HJWL, Miller T, Savova GK, Gallifant J,
Celi LA, Mak RH, Lustberg M, Afshar M, Bitterman DS.** "The effect of
using a large language model to respond to patient messages." *Lancet
Digital Health* 2024. PubMed: 38664108.

**Findings:**
- LLM responses **safe in 82.1%** of cases
- **58.3% acceptable to send unedited**
- **7.1% could pose risk to patient if left unedited**
- **0.6% could pose risk of death if left unedited**
- Physicians reported improved perceived efficiency

### 5. TRIPOD-LLM (Nature Medicine 2025)
**Gallifant J, Afshar M, Ameen S, ..., Bitterman DS.** "The TRIPOD-LLM
reporting guideline for studies using large language models." *Nature
Medicine* 31(1):60-69, January 2025.
DOI: 10.1038/s41591-024-03425-5.
Corresponding author: Bitterman. Lead author: Gallifant.

Nature Medicine: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-024-03425-5
PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39779929/
Interactive site: https://tripod-llm.vercel.app/

19 main items + 50 subitems, modular by 4 task categories
(classification, extraction, generation, conversational). 14 main
items + 32 subitems task-agnostic. Delphi process. Living document.

### 6. Toward Clinical-Grade Evaluation of Large Language Models (Int J Radiat Oncol Biol Phys 2024)
**Moreno AC, Bitterman DS.** *International Journal of Radiation
Oncology Biology Physics* 118(4):916-920, February 22, 2024.
DOI: 10.1016/j.ijrobp.2023.11.012.
PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11221761/

**Five-stage framework:**
1. Task definition (dual-annotated gold datasets, adjudication)
2. Methods development (lock model version, settings, prompt
   robustness)
3. Quantitative evaluation (automated metrics + acknowledge their
   limits)
4. Human evaluation (domain experts, blinded)
5. End-user studies (actual clinicians/patients under intended
   conditions)

### 7. Cross-Care (NeurIPS 2024)
**Chen S, Gallifant J, ..., Bitterman DS.** "Cross-Care: Assessing the
Healthcare Implications of Pre-training Data on Language Model Bias."
*NeurIPS 2024 Datasets and Benchmarks Track.*
https://neurips.cc/virtual/2024/poster/97819

Demographic distribution of disease-keyword co-occurrences in The Pile
mis-mirrors real-world disease prevalence. Alignment "minimally
resolves inconsistencies."

### 8. RABBITS (EMNLP Findings 2024)
**Gallifant J, Chen S, Moreira PJF, Munch N, Gao M, Pond J, Celi LA,
Aerts HJWL, Hartvigsen T, Bitterman D.** "Language Models are
Surprisingly Fragile to Drug Names in Biomedical Benchmarks."
*Findings of EMNLP 2024*, pp. 12448-12465.
arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.12066

Swap brand names for generics: **1-10% accuracy drops** in MedQA /
MedMCQA across models. Evidence of test-set contamination.

Repo: https://github.com/BittermanLab/RABBITS
Leaderboard: https://huggingface.co/spaces/AIM-Harvard/rabbits-leaderboard
