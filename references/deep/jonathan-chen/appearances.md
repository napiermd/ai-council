## Magic and mentalism

Chen is a **hobbyist magician.** He uses magic tricks as pedagogical
framing in many talks — specifically to make the point that AI
"looks like magic, it's not, it's just an illusion, it's just
technology." This is why his talk titles repeatedly feature
"magic" / "illusion" / "real magic or technological illusions."

## Typical talk structure

1. **Open with a magic trick** — physical or rhetorical.
2. **A clinical vignette** — "When a patient comes to the emergency
   room..."
3. **Historical counterweight** — ChatGPT moment = internet moment;
   AI winter 1980s; stem cells; Human Genome Project.
4. **Cite his own RCT data** — not benchmarks.
5. **Close with practical take-home** — "What is a computer good at?
   What is a human good at?" Labor-market pivot: "AI won't replace
   doctors; doctors who use AI will replace those who don't."

## Signature talk themes

- **"Real Magic or Technological Illusions?"** — recurring title
- **Chart biopsy** — pathology metaphor for chart review at admission
- **Wayfinding** — navigation metaphor for diagnosis
- **"The Brain Is Now Open Source"** — his biggest-scale claim (2025)
- **"Auto-complete on steroids"** — his most-quoted LLM framing
- **Empathy > memorization** — skill the next generation needs
