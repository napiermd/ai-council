## Signature artifacts

### Papers
- **NEJM 2017** — "Machine Learning and Prediction in Medicine —
  Beyond the Peak of Inflated Expectations." ~1,463 citations. The
  foundational essay.
- **JAMA 2021** — "Next-Generation AI for Diagnosis: From Predicting
  Diagnostic Labels to Wayfinding" (with Adler-Milstein, Dhaliwal).
- **JAMA Network Open 2024** — GPT-4 Diagnostic Reasoning RCT.
  Surprising finding: pairing did NOT help (P=0.60); LLM alone beat
  conventional resources.
- **Nature Medicine 2025** — GPT-4 Management Reasoning RCT. Pairing
  DID help (+6.5%, P<0.001); LLM alone ≈ pairing.
- **NEJM AI 2025** — MedAgentBench (Jiang, Black, Geng, Park, Zou,
  Ng, Chen). First benchmark requiring FHIR-compliant EHR interaction.
- **arXiv 2509.05878 (2025)** — MedAgentBrief + MedFactEval. With
  Emily Alsentzer and Nigam Shah. Production deployment: 384 discharges,
  57% used AI content, 2% hallucinations.
- **npj Digital Medicine 2026** — "From Tool to Teammate" RCT.
  Collaborative workflows designed deliberately finally beat traditional
  tools (AI-first 85%, AI-second 82%, Traditional 75%, AI alone 90%).
- **JAMA Intern Med 2016** — Opioid distribution paper (Chen first
  author; Shah co-author). Top 10% of prescribers account for 57% of
  opioid prescriptions — but same pattern holds for all drugs.

### Tools
- **OrderRex** — clinical decision support that queries the collective
  experience of prior similar patients. His signature tool. Origin
  insight: *"Why look at just one person's chart? Why not look at the
  last thousand charts?"*

### Benchmarks
- **MedAgentBench** (2025) — 300 clinical tasks, FHIR-compliant EHR
  environment, 100 patient profiles, 700K+ data elements. Best
  original-release model: Claude 3.5 Sonnet v2, 69.67%. MedAgentBench
  v2 with GPT-4.1 + memory: 98.0%.
- **MedFactEval** — LLM jury evaluation for clinical summaries. κ=0.81
  with physician panel.
- **NOHARM** — "Numerous Options Harm Assessment for Risk in Medicine"
  (verified expansion; my earlier prompt had it wrong). Measures
  clinical harm, not just accuracy.
