# Example: Product Direction Review

## When to use

Use this when you are trying to decide whether a product direction is real,
premature, or overbuilt.

## Example prompt

```text
Use the AI Council to pressure-test this product direction.
I want the sharpest 3 voices only.

Decision:
Should I build an AI workspace that turns scattered founder notes, meetings,
and strategy fragments into a live operating system for execution?

Goal:
Create something founders use every day, not just a demo they admire once.

Current direction:
I am leaning toward a fast, opinionated product with strong memory, workflow
prompts, and a tight founder UX rather than a broad general-purpose agent.

Context:
Early-stage product. Solo founder. Need to ship quickly. Product quality and
retention matter more than research novelty.

Constraints:
- Time: short
- Budget: limited
- Team: solo
- Stack: LLM APIs plus product layer
- Data: user notes, docs, meetings
- Reliability / safety: important, but not regulated

What I am unsure about:
- Whether the product is too broad
- Whether memory should be the core wedge
- Whether I should narrow harder around a single workflow

What I want from the council:
- Tell me which voices should be active
- Show me the core tension
- Give me the recommendation
- Tell me what to do next
```

## Recommended voices

- Pete Steinberger
- Chip Huyen
- Jeremy Howard

## Why these voices

- Steinberger forces product sharpness and cuts fake scope.
- Huyen checks whether the system will behave reliably in practice.
- Howard keeps the plan grounded in fast, testable iteration.
