# Example: Academic Memo Response

## Situation

You are not writing a survey of agent systems. You are trying to make a clear
claim about whether current LLM-based agents create durable organizational
productivity gains under real operating conditions.

## Active voices

- Chris Olah
- Fei-Fei Li
- Jeremy Howard
- Chip Huyen

## Core tension

The tension is between visible capability and durable organizational value.

Agent systems can look impressive in bounded demos while still failing to
produce sustained gains once evaluation, workflow fit, error handling, and
human trust enter the picture.

## What each voice is saying

- Olah: Be precise about what counts as an agent and what counts as a durable gain. Do not hide uncertainty behind broad language.
- Fei-Fei Li: The organizational setting matters. The memo should not treat technical capability as separable from the human environment of use.
- Howard: Cut the abstraction. Ask what evidence would convince you that agents help in practice and what baseline they must beat.
- Huyen: The strongest version of your argument should foreground evaluation, observability, and degradation under real use.

## Recommendation

Sharpen the memo around this claim:

Current LLM-based agents show real local utility, but claims of durable
organization-level productivity gains are premature unless workflow fit,
evaluation, and failure handling are demonstrated explicitly.

## Next move

Rewrite the memo so each paragraph supports one of three pillars:
- local capability is real
- durable organizational gain is harder than demo performance
- evaluation and workflow evidence are the actual bar
